# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Residential and commercial property owners in Ormoc City, Leyte, and the broader Visayas region, Philippines. They are considering solar for the first time or have an existing system that needs servicing. Context: comparing installers, often on mobile, looking for a trustworthy local company — not a distant corporation. Job to be done: quickly establish confidence in JMC's expertise, then take the next step (book a consultation or assessment).

Secondary: returning customers booking maintenance or follow-up services.

## Product Purpose

JMC Solar PH is a licensed solar installation company based in Ormoc City, Leyte. The site exists to convert visitors into booked consultations, site assessments, or maintenance appointments. Success looks like a local property owner feeling confident enough to book — and a company that clearly stands apart from generic, unverified solar vendors.

Slogan: "Future is Electric."

Services: hybrid solar, on-grid systems, battery energy storage, EV chargers, solar water pumping, net metering, O&M maintenance.

## Brand Personality

Expert & trustworthy, local & community-rooted, bold & forward-looking.

Three-word anchor: **Grounded. Electric. Ours.**

Voice: direct, technically confident, warm without being informal. Speaks like a licensed engineer who grew up in Leyte — not a startup pitch deck. No hype. No eco-nonprofit softness.

## Anti-references

- Generic green eco-brand: leaf icons, soft sage greens, friendly-nonprofit palette. JMC is a technical trade business, not a wellness brand.
- Loud solar stock-photo sites: neon yellow CTAs, clip-art panel imagery, discount-heavy visual language.
- Generic AI slop: eyebrow labels on every section, identical card grids, gradient text, hero-metric templates, Inter-for-everything flatness.
- Western minimalism: sterile all-white sterility that feels foreign to a Filipino local business. Warmth and confidence must coexist.

## Design Principles

1. **Local authority, not global template.** Every choice — typeface, color weight, layout rhythm — should feel made for Ormoc City, not replicated from a SaaS kit. If a stranger could paste this UI onto a US startup without changing anything, it's wrong.

2. **Engineer's confidence.** Design should communicate deep technical knowledge without shouting. Precision over decoration. When showing specs, show them cleanly. When showing trust signals, earn them with substance.

3. **Bold and grounded.** "Future is Electric" is assertive. The visual tone matches: strong color decisions, purposeful weight, not timid. But grounded in the trade — never fashion-forward for its own sake.

4. **Progress as clarity.** The booking flow is the site's primary conversion path. Every step must feel inevitable and clear. Confusion kills bookings; elegance earns them.

5. **Community-first scale.** This is a local business. Scale the voice and layout accordingly: a Filipino homeowner in Leyte should feel spoken to directly, not broadcast at.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Full keyboard navigation. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Mobile-first — a large portion of the target audience accesses via mobile on Philippine mobile data.
